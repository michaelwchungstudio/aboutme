Michael Chung (b. 1992) is an artist and freelance designer currently living in Philadelphia, PA. His work is largely an extension of his interests in the philosophies of life (existentialism, metaphysics, mortality), psychology, and Internet culture and communities. Through constructing and/or altering spaces using light, digital media, and fragile, often transparent or reflective, materials, he aims to develop a meaningful physical relationship with the viewer. Chung's most recent project, i feel more comfortable (2016), consisted of a datamoshed video displayed behind a two-way mirror, distorting the viewer's reflection. The installation explored the societal and interpersonal impact of 'Internet connections', focusing on aspects such as asynchronicity, anonymity, removal of physical presence, and scope of audience.
